## The role

Samsung Electronics Turkey was my entry into serious marketing — a global brand, a large domestic market, and a product portfolio that spanned TV, home appliances, monitors, and memory. I joined as a Retail Marketing and Store Development Specialist and moved into Digital Marketing after two years, ultimately running 100+ campaigns across four product divisions.

Four years at Samsung is a long time in a market that moves quickly. I learned what rigorous multi-channel campaign execution looks like, what good creative briefing requires, and how to make a campaign do something culturally interesting rather than just competent.

## What I actually did

### The Göbeklitepe campaign

The work I'm proudest of from this period started with a brief that most people would have done predictably. Samsung was launching a new TV range. The brief was to demonstrate picture quality. The obvious execution: show a beautiful landscape in high resolution.

I took it somewhere else. Göbeklitepe is one of the oldest archaeological sites in the world — a UNESCO heritage site in southeastern Turkey that most Turkish people knew vaguely but had never seen properly documented. I proposed using it as the demonstration canvas: a 360-degree cinematic production of the site, shot on Samsung's own equipment, giving Turkish consumers a view of their own heritage they'd never had access to.

The campaign won three Kristal Elma Awards in 2019. More importantly, the YouTube product comparison format we developed alongside it was adopted as the Samsung EMEA benchmark — meaning a campaign born in the Turkish market became the template for the region.

### 150 stores in 2 years

Before moving to digital, I spent two years in retail marketing — specifically directing the national rollout of Samsung brand experience stores across Turkey. 150 stores in two years is a logistics and coordination challenge as much as a marketing one.

The meaningful part was the commercial result: +20% sales per square meter, achieved through a systematic redesign of the in-store display and merchandising architecture. The insight was simple but under-applied: the way a product is displayed determines whether a customer considers it, and most retail setups optimise for how many SKUs fit on a shelf rather than how customers actually navigate a decision.

### Running four divisions simultaneously

The Digital Marketing Specialist role covered four product categories simultaneously — TV, home appliances, monitors, and memory. That's four different audiences, four different purchase triggers, four different competitor landscapes, and a single team managing all of them.

The discipline it required — being able to context-switch between a high-involvement considered purchase (a premium TV) and a low-involvement commodity category (memory) and apply different strategic logic to each — is something I use constantly. The consumer electronics category doesn't let you get lazy with segmentation.
