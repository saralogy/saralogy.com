## The role

VeSync is the EU growth organisation behind COSORI, Levoit, and Etekcity — consumer electronics brands that compete in the crowded kitchen appliance and air quality categories on Amazon, retail, and D2C. I joined as Integrated Marketing Manager for the German lead market with ownership of the full channel mix and a category GMV plan that covered everything from sell-out targets to retail media allocations.

The role was unusual in its scope: full P&L accountability for German market performance, a team spanning Germany and China HQ, and a fixed-term contract structure that meant every launch had to count. I ran three major product launches in six months.

## What I actually did

### The airfryer launch: 117% in 53 days

The flagship launch was a premium airfryer in a category that was already crowded and commoditising. The brief was clear — hit sell-out in a 53-day window. The challenge: a higher price point than the category average, a new SKU with no review history, and a German consumer who researches extensively before buying anything over €100.

The strategy was built around the Jobs-to-Be-Done framework. We ran consumer interviews to map the actual purchase trigger — not "I want a healthier lifestyle" (that's the noise), but "I have a specific cooking problem that this solves better than my current workaround." The insights shaped the creative brief, the keyword strategy, and the influencer selection simultaneously.

Result: 117% of sell-out target. 4.0% conversion against a 2.5% category benchmark. 7 channels active simultaneously, each brief built from the same Jobs-to-Be-Done foundation.

### The TikTok tower fan launch

For the Levoit tower fan, the brief required something different — a seasonal product that most consumers don't think about until a heatwave makes it urgent. I built a TikTok-first launch with weather-triggered media: ads activated automatically when temperature forecasts crossed a threshold in targeted cities.

The activation included a 7-stage consumer journey mapped from TikTok discovery through to in-store conversion, plus a 3-city retail café activation that created physical brand presence alongside the digital campaign. It was the kind of integrated campaign that requires tight coordination across in-house, agency, and retail partners — all of which I managed without a formal team under me.

### The governance problem

Running launches across Germany and China HQ without line authority is a coordination problem as much as a marketing one. The standard failure mode is that German market needs and China HQ priorities get out of sync, and the campaign brief drifts between briefing and execution.

I addressed this by building a shared launch governance framework — a single source of truth for campaign objectives, channel allocations, and approval stages that both sides could work from. I then used the same framework to onboard the Spain and France marketing managers, so the model could scale beyond Germany.

### Building the playbooks

Alongside the launches, I built three full-funnel launch playbooks: one per product category, each covering product differentiation framework, price positioning logic, customer segmentation approach, and channel priority matrix. All three were validated through Jobs-to-Be-Done testing before being used in live campaigns.

The goal was to make the next launch faster and better than the previous one — to build institutional knowledge in a company that had historically run launches reactively. Each playbook was designed to be handed off and improved by whoever came next.
