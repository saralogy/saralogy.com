An instrumental piece built from the feeling of walking through Hamburg at night. No lyrics, no drums, no conventional song structure. Just synth layers exploring a single harmonic space for five minutes.

The piece was written over a month of evening walks. I was listening for the city's sound—not traffic, not noise, but the specific texture of a northern European city after midnight. Train whistles in the distance. Water movement. Footsteps on wet pavement.

The production is intentionally minimal: 11 layers of sustained synth, each adding texture rather than melody. The goal was to build something that sounds like nothing else you've heard, but feels familiar. The reverb is part of the composition, not an effect applied after.

Listen on headphones at night. This is a piece for 2 AM, not for playlists.

Available on Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, and as a high-res download for people with good audio equipment.
