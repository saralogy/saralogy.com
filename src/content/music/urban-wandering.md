A song about being in motion—physically between cities, mentally between states. The production is intentionally layered but sparse: you hear the guitar foundation in the verses, then the synth opens everything up in the chorus.

The song was written over three weeks, mostly at night. The lyrics came from a walking habit through Hamburg—noticing patterns in the city that no one else seems to see, or maybe everyone sees but no one talks about.

Production was deliberate: home studio, three vocal takes, heavy reliance on spatial effects to make the small room feel bigger. The synth is the main character in the second half of the song; the goal was to make it feel like it's arriving to answer a question the verses asked.

Listen on Spotify, Apple Music, or YouTube. The drum pattern is custom MIDI paired with sampled breaks—took longer to get right than the melody.
